Elizabeth's

“The Game:The Game: does an excellent job at giving a player an introduction into the warped world of the pick up artist community. Not only that, but it serves as a genuinely enjoyable experience as its visuals, variation in choice, and extensive storyline blend together seamlessly into creating an eerie atmosphere that gives the player a glimpse into the misogynist culture that suffocates many women. However, the real horror is in the realization that everything we experience in the game is based entirely on real, prominent figures within the pick up community and their hoards of loyal supporters. That is the true spine chilling revelation as you begin to look deeper and see the painstaking effort put in by Washko to create an accurate, and realistic depiction of the men who subject women to this same treatment.
