In-game Elements

Taking as long as four years to make, Washko has carefully crafted “The Game: The Game” to accurately depict the scary realities behind how pick-up culture is. Each artistic element of the game contributes to the message she tried portraying in an incredibly effective and meaningful manner.

Artwork

  • The backgrounds are drawings done on cyanotype-based backgrounds
  • This type of artwork gives a paper-like texture and is coupled with a minimal color palette
  • References the aesthetics of detective or pulp novels where gendered violence often occurs

Music

  • Many interactions with the pick-up artists eventually would lead to you feeling “trapped” in conversation or a possible sense of obligation to keep the conversation going, even if refusing was an option
  • The soundtrack of the game consists of an eerie ambiance that tries to emulate this continous feeling of suffocation and “doom” as you continue each encounter with each pick-up artist

Range of Choices

  • In each conversation, the player has a range of multiple choices of dialogue when responding to the pick-up artists
  • Sometimes you are just choosing between what to say in return to small talk but often times when the player starts to get into an uncomfortable conversation the player is able to choose from dialogue that ranges in showing how much you enjoy the conversation and how confrontational you want to be
  • This meant that oftentimes, the player is able to pick dialogue that either:
    1. Portrays them wanting to go along with a conversation
    2. Reluctantly going along with the conversation
    3. Being neutral and saying nothing
    4. Trying to get out of the conversation in a friendly approach
    5. Being blunt and confrontational (and even sometimes rude) to get out of the situation
  • In this way, you are given freedom to choose you approach in how you attempt to navigate and defuse uncomfortable and/or threatning situations.

Range of Interactions

  • In the game, you encounter a variety of interactions that you will have to go through:
    • Small talk
    • Flirting
    • Dancing
    • Hostile conversations/situations
    • Going to places outide of the bar (i.e. ice cream shop, another club, a guy's house, etc.)
    • PDA/affection
    • Sex scenes
  • Through these conversations, Wahko tries to depict how pick-up artists (and men with similar mindsets) see social interaction as a contest and see women as a sexual conquest who will often times overstep boundaries to do accomplish this.
